# Requirements Document - Accounting Module

## Introduction

This document defines the requirements for a professional accounting module integrated into the existing POS/ERP system. The module will provide comprehensive financial management capabilities including journal entries, general ledger, financial statements, accounts receivable/payable, and automated accounting entries from sales and purchase transactions. The system will support multi-branch operations with role-based access control and provide real-time financial insights.

## Glossary

- **Accounting_Module**: The complete accounting subsystem that manages financial transactions, ledgers, and reports
- **Chart_of_Accounts**: The structured list of all accounts used to categorize financial transactions
- **Account**: A unique financial category in the chart of accounts (e.g., Cash, Sales Revenue, Inventory)
- **Journal_Entry**: A record of a financial transaction with debits and credits that must balance
- **General_Ledger**: The complete record of all financial transactions organized by account
- **Balance_Sheet**: Financial statement showing assets, liabilities, and equity at a specific point in time
- **Income_Statement**: Financial statement showing revenues, expenses, and profit/loss over a period
- **Cash_Flow_Statement**: Financial statement showing cash inflows and outflows over a period
- **Accounts_Receivable**: Money owed to the business by customers
- **Accounts_Payable**: Money owed by the business to suppliers
- **Fiscal_Period**: A defined accounting period (monthly, quarterly, or annual)
- **Closing_Process**: The procedure to finalize accounts at the end of a fiscal period
- **Cost_Center**: A branch or department for which costs are tracked separately
- **Bank_Reconciliation**: The process of matching bank statement transactions with accounting records
- **Automated_Entry**: A journal entry created automatically by the system from a business transaction
- **Manual_Entry**: A journal entry created directly by an authorized user
- **Transaction_Source**: The origin of a financial transaction (POS Sale, Purchase Order, Manual Entry, etc.)
- **Financial_Report**: Any generated document showing financial data (statements, ledgers, summaries)
- **User**: Any authenticated person using the system with assigned role and permissions
- **Branch**: A physical location or cost center within the business
- **Business**: The parent organization that owns one or more branches

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: Chart of Accounts Management

**User Story:** As an accounting manager, I want to define and maintain a structured chart of accounts, so that all financial transactions can be properly categorized and reported.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Accounting_Module SHALL provide a hierarchical chart of accounts with account types: Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Revenue, Expenses
2. WHEN a user with OWNER or MANAGER role creates an account, THE Accounting_Module SHALL require account code, name, type, and parent account (if applicable)
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL enforce unique account codes within the business
4. WHEN an account has child accounts, THE Accounting_Module SHALL prevent deletion of the parent account
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support account activation and deactivation without deletion
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL maintain account balance history for all active accounts
7. WHEN displaying the chart of accounts, THE Accounting_Module SHALL show accounts in hierarchical order with indentation levels

### Requirement 2: Manual Journal Entry Creation

**User Story:** As an accountant, I want to create manual journal entries, so that I can record financial transactions that are not automatically generated by the system.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a user with OWNER or MANAGER role creates a manual journal entry, THE Accounting_Module SHALL require entry date, description, and at least two line items
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL enforce that total debits equal total credits for every journal entry
3. WHEN a journal entry is saved, THE Accounting_Module SHALL assign a unique sequential entry number
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL record the user who created the entry and the timestamp
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support attaching supporting documents to journal entries
6. WHEN a journal entry is created, THE Accounting_Module SHALL update the general ledger immediately
7. THE Accounting_Module SHALL allow journal entry reversal by creating an offsetting entry with reference to the original

### Requirement 3: Automated Journal Entries from Sales

**User Story:** As a business owner, I want the system to automatically create accounting entries when sales occur, so that financial records are always current without manual data entry.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a sale is completed in the POS, THE Accounting_Module SHALL automatically create a journal entry within 5 seconds
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL debit Accounts_Receivable or Cash based on payment method
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL credit Sales_Revenue for the sale amount
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL debit Cost_of_Goods_Sold and credit Inventory for the product cost
5. WHEN a sale includes multiple payment methods, THE Accounting_Module SHALL create separate debit entries for each payment method
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL tag automated entries with the source transaction ID and type
7. WHEN a sale is voided, THE Accounting_Module SHALL create reversing entries that reference the original sale

### Requirement 4: Automated Journal Entries from Purchases

**User Story:** As an inventory manager, I want purchase orders to automatically generate accounting entries, so that inventory and payables are accurately tracked.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a purchase order status changes to RECEIVED, THE Accounting_Module SHALL create a journal entry within 5 seconds
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL debit Inventory for the total purchase cost
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL credit Accounts_Payable for the amount owed to the supplier
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL tag the entry with the purchase order ID and supplier information
5. WHEN a purchase order is cancelled, THE Accounting_Module SHALL create reversing entries if the order was previously received
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support partial receipts by creating proportional journal entries

### Requirement 5: General Ledger

**User Story:** As an accountant, I want to view the general ledger for any account, so that I can see all transactions affecting that account over time.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a user requests the general ledger for an account, THE Accounting_Module SHALL display all journal entry line items for that account
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL show entry date, description, debit amount, credit amount, running balance, and source reference
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support filtering by date range, transaction source, and branch
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate and display opening balance, total debits, total credits, and closing balance
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support exporting ledger data to Excel and PDF formats
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL display entries in chronological order with most recent first
7. WHEN viewing a ledger entry, THE Accounting_Module SHALL provide a link to view the complete journal entry

### Requirement 6: Balance Sheet Generation

**User Story:** As a business owner, I want to generate a balance sheet, so that I can see the financial position of my business at any point in time.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a user requests a balance sheet, THE Accounting_Module SHALL require a specific date
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate total assets by summing all asset account balances as of the specified date
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate total liabilities by summing all liability account balances as of the specified date
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate total equity by summing all equity account balances as of the specified date
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL verify that Assets equal Liabilities plus Equity
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL display accounts grouped by type with subtotals
7. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support filtering by branch to generate branch-specific balance sheets
8. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support exporting balance sheets to PDF and Excel formats

### Requirement 7: Income Statement Generation

**User Story:** As a manager, I want to generate an income statement, so that I can analyze revenues, expenses, and profitability over a specific period.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a user requests an income statement, THE Accounting_Module SHALL require start date and end date
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate total revenue by summing all revenue account transactions within the period
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate total expenses by summing all expense account transactions within the period
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate gross profit as revenue minus cost of goods sold
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate net profit as total revenue minus total expenses
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL display accounts grouped by category with subtotals
7. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support filtering by branch to generate branch-specific income statements
8. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support comparative periods showing current period versus previous period
9. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support exporting income statements to PDF and Excel formats

### Requirement 8: Cash Flow Statement Generation

**User Story:** As a financial analyst, I want to generate a cash flow statement, so that I can understand how cash moves through the business.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a user requests a cash flow statement, THE Accounting_Module SHALL require start date and end date
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL categorize cash flows into operating activities, investing activities, and financing activities
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate net cash from operating activities based on revenue and expense transactions
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate net cash from investing activities based on asset purchases and sales
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate net cash from financing activities based on equity and debt transactions
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL display opening cash balance, total cash inflows, total cash outflows, and closing cash balance
7. THE Accounting_Module SHALL verify that closing cash balance matches the cash account balance in the general ledger
8. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support exporting cash flow statements to PDF and Excel formats

### Requirement 9: Accounts Receivable Management

**User Story:** As a credit manager, I want to track accounts receivable, so that I can monitor customer payments and outstanding balances.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a sale is made on credit, THE Accounting_Module SHALL create an accounts receivable record for the customer
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL display all outstanding receivables grouped by customer
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL show invoice date, due date, amount, and aging (days overdue)
4. WHEN a customer payment is received, THE Accounting_Module SHALL reduce the receivable balance and create a journal entry
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support partial payments and track remaining balance
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL generate aging reports showing receivables by age brackets (0-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+ days)
7. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate total outstanding receivables across all customers

### Requirement 10: Accounts Payable Management

**User Story:** As a purchasing manager, I want to track accounts payable, so that I can manage supplier payments and cash flow.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a purchase is made on credit, THE Accounting_Module SHALL create an accounts payable record for the supplier
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL display all outstanding payables grouped by supplier
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL show purchase date, due date, amount, and aging (days overdue)
4. WHEN a supplier payment is made, THE Accounting_Module SHALL reduce the payable balance and create a journal entry
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support partial payments and track remaining balance
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL generate aging reports showing payables by age brackets (0-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+ days)
7. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate total outstanding payables across all suppliers

### Requirement 11: Bank Reconciliation

**User Story:** As an accountant, I want to reconcile bank statements with accounting records, so that I can ensure accuracy and identify discrepancies.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a user initiates bank reconciliation, THE Accounting_Module SHALL require selection of bank account and statement period
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL display all unreconciled transactions for the selected account
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL allow marking transactions as cleared when they appear on the bank statement
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate the difference between book balance and bank statement balance
5. WHEN all transactions are reconciled, THE Accounting_Module SHALL verify that the difference is zero
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support adding adjusting entries for bank fees, interest, or errors
7. THE Accounting_Module SHALL save reconciliation status and prevent modification of reconciled transactions
8. THE Accounting_Module SHALL generate a reconciliation report showing cleared and uncleared items

### Requirement 12: Multi-Branch Cost Center Tracking

**User Story:** As a business owner with multiple branches, I want to track financial performance by branch, so that I can compare profitability across locations.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a journal entry is created, THE Accounting_Module SHALL require assignment to a branch cost center
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL tag all automated entries with the branch where the transaction occurred
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support generating financial reports filtered by single branch or multiple branches
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate branch-specific profit and loss
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support consolidation of all branches into a single business-wide report
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL display branch comparison reports showing key metrics side-by-side
7. WHEN a user has access to multiple branches, THE Accounting_Module SHALL default to their assigned branch but allow selection of others

### Requirement 13: Fiscal Period Closing

**User Story:** As an accounting manager, I want to close fiscal periods, so that financial data is locked and year-end processes can be completed.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a user with OWNER role initiates period closing, THE Accounting_Module SHALL require confirmation and period selection
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL verify that all transactions in the period are properly recorded
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL calculate closing balances for all accounts
4. WHEN closing is complete, THE Accounting_Module SHALL prevent creation or modification of transactions in the closed period
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL transfer revenue and expense account balances to retained earnings
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL create opening balances for the next period based on closing balances
7. THE Accounting_Module SHALL generate a closing report showing all closing entries and final balances
8. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support reopening a closed period with proper authorization and audit trail

### Requirement 14: Financial Report Dashboard

**User Story:** As a manager, I want a financial dashboard, so that I can quickly see key financial metrics and trends.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Accounting_Module SHALL display a dashboard with key financial indicators: total revenue, total expenses, net profit, cash balance
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL show current month performance compared to previous month
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL display revenue and expense trends over the last 12 months as a chart
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL show accounts receivable and accounts payable summaries
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL display top revenue-generating branches
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL update dashboard metrics in real-time as transactions occur
7. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support filtering dashboard by date range and branch

### Requirement 15: Role-Based Access Control

**User Story:** As a system administrator, I want to control who can access accounting features, so that sensitive financial data is protected.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Accounting_Module SHALL restrict access to users with roles: SUPER_ADMIN, OWNER, MANAGER
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL allow OWNER role to perform all accounting operations including period closing
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL allow MANAGER role to create manual entries and view reports but not close periods
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL allow SUPER_ADMIN role to view all accounting data across all businesses for audit purposes
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL prevent CASHIER, SELLER, and USER roles from accessing accounting features
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL log all accounting operations with user identification for audit trail
7. WHEN an unauthorized user attempts to access accounting features, THE Accounting_Module SHALL display an access denied message

### Requirement 16: Audit Trail and Transaction History

**User Story:** As an auditor, I want to see a complete history of all accounting transactions, so that I can verify accuracy and detect irregularities.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Accounting_Module SHALL record every journal entry with timestamp, user, and source
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL maintain a permanent log of all modifications to journal entries
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL track reversals and adjustments with references to original entries
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support filtering audit logs by date range, user, account, and transaction type
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL display audit trail in chronological order with most recent first
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support exporting audit logs to Excel and PDF formats
7. THE Accounting_Module SHALL prevent deletion of audit log records

### Requirement 17: Mobile-Responsive Accounting Interface

**User Story:** As a manager on the go, I want to access accounting reports on my mobile device, so that I can monitor financial performance from anywhere.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Accounting_Module SHALL provide a mobile-optimized interface for screens smaller than 768 pixels wide
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL display financial reports in a readable format on mobile devices
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support touch gestures for navigation and filtering
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL use the same visual style as other mobile pages (rounded cards, slate colors, hugeicons)
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL lazy-load desktop components only when accessed from desktop devices
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL provide a bottom navigation option for quick access to key accounting features on mobile
7. WHEN viewing reports on mobile, THE Accounting_Module SHALL support horizontal scrolling for wide tables

### Requirement 18: Integration with Existing POS/ERP Data

**User Story:** As a system integrator, I want the accounting module to seamlessly integrate with existing sales, purchases, and inventory data, so that financial records are automatically synchronized.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Accounting_Module SHALL read sales data from the existing Sales table without modification
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL read purchase data from the existing PurchaseOrder table without modification
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL read inventory movements from the existing StockMovement table without modification
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL read cash session data from the existing CashSession table without modification
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL create journal entries based on existing transaction data
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL maintain referential integrity with source transactions through foreign keys
7. WHEN source data is modified or voided, THE Accounting_Module SHALL automatically create adjusting entries

### Requirement 19: Export and Reporting Capabilities

**User Story:** As an accountant, I want to export financial data in multiple formats, so that I can share reports with stakeholders and import data into other systems.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support exporting all financial reports to PDF format
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support exporting all financial reports to Excel format
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support exporting journal entries to CSV format for import into external accounting software
4. WHEN exporting to PDF, THE Accounting_Module SHALL include business logo, report title, date range, and page numbers
5. WHEN exporting to Excel, THE Accounting_Module SHALL preserve formatting and formulas
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL generate export files within 10 seconds for reports containing up to 10,000 transactions
7. THE Accounting_Module SHALL provide a download link or automatic download when export is complete

### Requirement 20: Performance and Scalability

**User Story:** As a system administrator, I want the accounting module to perform efficiently, so that users experience fast response times even with large transaction volumes.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Accounting_Module SHALL load the main accounting dashboard within 2 seconds
2. THE Accounting_Module SHALL generate financial reports within 5 seconds for periods containing up to 10,000 transactions
3. THE Accounting_Module SHALL create automated journal entries within 5 seconds of the source transaction
4. THE Accounting_Module SHALL support concurrent access by up to 50 users without performance degradation
5. THE Accounting_Module SHALL use database indexes on frequently queried fields (account_id, entry_date, branch_id)
6. THE Accounting_Module SHALL implement pagination for lists displaying more than 100 items
7. THE Accounting_Module SHALL cache frequently accessed data such as chart of accounts for 5 minutes
